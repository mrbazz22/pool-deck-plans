Pool Deck Stake & String Layout Guide

One Simple 22'×12' Rectangle — v11.5 · May 17, 2026

📐 Layout Philosophy

One rectangle. One continuous frame. No center seam. No separate sections. You are laying out a single 22' × 12' rectangle. The picture-frame border in the decking creates the visual detail — it does NOT need any special framing layout.

01Tools Needed

  • 4 wooden stakes (2×2 or similar, 18"+ long)
  • 100' tape measure
  • String line (mason's line or twine)
  • Spray paint (marking paint for grass/dirt)
  • Speed square
  • Hammer
  • Line level (optional but helpful)

02Step-by-Step Layout

You are laying out ONE 22' × 12' rectangle. The pool is 24' round and sits centered on the deck (or behind it). Adjust based on your actual pool position.

  1. Mark the back edge. Drive two stakes (A and B) 22' apart. This is your back rim line. Run a string between them at ground level. Use spray paint to mark the line on the ground.
  2. Square the corners. From stake A, measure 12' forward (perpendicular to the back edge) and place stake C. Use the 3-4-5 method or a speed square to ensure a 90° angle: measure 9' along the back string from A, 12' toward C, and the diagonal should be exactly 15'. Adjust C until it is square.
  3. Complete the rectangle. From stake B, measure 12' forward parallel to AC, and place stake D. Check the diagonal BD — it should equal AC (both ~25' 0¾"). Adjust until both diagonals match. Run strings CD and AD to complete the 22' × 12' rectangle.
  4. Verify square. Measure both diagonals (A to D, B to C). They must be equal. For a 22' × 12' rectangle, diagonals = √(22² + 12²) = √(484 + 144) = √628 ≈ 25.06' (25' 0¾"). If diagonals differ by more than 1", adjust corners until they match.
  5. Mark the frame perimeter. Use spray paint to mark the full rectangle on the ground, 6" inside the string lines (accounting for the 5.5" rim + a small working margin). This is your excavation/grading boundary.
  6. Check for level. Use a line level or set a laser level on the back string. Check the front string and both side strings. The goal is within ±1" of level across the entire 22' × 12' area. If needed, scrape high spots and fill low spots with crushed stone or compacted gravel.

⚠️ Do Not Mark a Center Line for Framing
There is no center seam in this design. Do NOT drive a stake or run a string down the middle for framing purposes. The frame is one solid 22' × 12' piece. The picture-frame border is installed during decking — it doesn't need a center reference line.

06Site Prep Checklist

  1. Clear all vegetation and debris within 23' × 12' area (1' working margin on all sides).
  2. Rough-grade with rake and shovel — within ±1" of level across full 22' × 12' area.
  3. Spread 4–5 tons crushed stone (¾" or smaller) OR place flat stone pavers at pedestal locations.
  4. Tamp stone well — no soft spots under pedestals.
  5. Mark pedestal locations with spray paint as shown above.
  6. Install pedestals, adjusting threaded barrels so tops are roughly at final height (~7" above grade).
  7. Place temporary 2×6 scraps on pedestals and check level with a long level or laser. Adjust individual pedestals up/down until the surface is flat.
💡 Pro Tip: Level in Stages
Don't try to get every pedestal perfect in one pass. Set them all roughly, then place the assembled frame on top and fine-tune from above. It's much easier to adjust a pedestal when you can see exactly where the frame needs to go.
