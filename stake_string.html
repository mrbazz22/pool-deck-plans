2Find Your Reference Point

Every measurement starts from one fixed point. For this deck, the best reference is the pool coping (the rim). It's already level, round, and won't move.

💡 Mark the center of the pool first · Measure across the coping in 2 directions (like a + sign) · Where they cross = center · Drive a temporary stake · All radial measurements start here

4Step-by-Step Layout

Step 1: Mark the Back Edge (Stake A → Stake B)

1
Decide where the back of the deck goes. This is where the step will be. Usually ~12' from the pool coping.
2
Drive Stake A at left corner of back edge. Use mallet until firm.
3
Measure 22' exactly to the right. Drive Stake B. Tie string A→B tight at ground level.
4
Verify: String should be straight, taut, touching ground. Spray paint along string for visible line.

Step 2: Mark the Left & Right Sides (Perpendicular from A & B)

5
At Stake A, use the 3-4-5 triangle method to find a perpendicular line:
  • Measure 3' along string A→B, mark with tape or thumb
  • From that mark, measure 4' out into yard (away from pool)
  • Adjust until the diagonal = exactly 5'
  • That's your 90° angle — direction of left side
6
Drive Stake D at ~11.5' from A along that perpendicular. Tie string A→D.
7
Repeat at Stake B — 3-4-5 triangle right side. Drive Stake C at ~11.5'. Tie string B→C.
💡 Pro tip: Instead of 3-4-5, you can also use a framing square or measure diagonals: A→C and B→D should be exactly equal (both ~28' 6"). If they're not, your rectangle isn't square.

Step 3: Mark the Center Seam (Between Sections)

8
Find the midpoint of string A→B (at 11' from either end). Drive a temporary stake here — this is the center seam line.
9
Measure 3" left and 3" right of this midpoint. These mark the edges of the 6" center seam.
10
Drive Stake E at 11' from A (and 11' from B). Tie a perpendicular string from the back edge to the front edge through this point. This string marks the inner edge of both sections' right/left borders.
💡 Center seam: The 6" gap between sections is not a structural joint — it's a visual seam filled with a perpendicular decking strip. The doubled rim joists on each section's inner edge provide the structural support.

Step 4: Mark the Double Border Inset

💡 Picture-frame border: Each section has a double-row border (2 rows of 5/4×6 boards = 11" total depth) on all four sides. The inner field (where boards run left-to-right) starts 11" inside from each edge.
11
From each section's outer perimeter, measure 11" inward on all four sides. Mark with spray paint:
  • Back edge: 11" forward from A→B string
  • Front edge: 11" back from front rim line
  • Left/right: 11" in from outer side strings
  • Center seam: 11" out from seam string on both sides
12
The resulting inner rectangle for each section = 9' 2" × 10' 2". This is your field board area. The border boards run perpendicular (front-to-back on sides, left-to-right on front/back).
⚠️ Double-check: The inner field should be consistent on all sides. If one side measures differently, your rectangle isn't square. Re-check with diagonals.

6Grading & Site Prep

What to Remove

  • Grass and topsoil (first 2–3 inches)
  • Roots, rocks, stumps inside the footprint
  • Anything organic that will decompose and settle

What to Add

  • Excavate 4–6" below finished grade · Remove topsoil and organic material
  • Landscape fabric: Woven geotextile over native soil (prevents migration)
  • Crushed stone: 2–3 tons of ¾" stone in 2" lifts · Plate compactor between lifts
  • Flat stone pavers: One under each pedestal location (more stable than gravel alone)
  • Combo base (recommended): Flat stone at each pedestal + compacted gravel between

Level Check

1
Lay a straight 2×6 across the footprint in multiple directions.
2
Check with a level. Goal: ±1" across entire 22' × ~12' area. Slight slope away from pool for drainage is OK.
3
If low spots: add stone and tamp with plate compactor in 2" lifts. If high spots: dig out or scrape down.
4
Tamp everything firm. Walk on it — should feel solid, not spongy.
⚠️ Critical: The stone base is what carries the load. Pedestals on soft ground = deck sinks. Take your time here.

7Removing Strings & Stakes

Strings are temporary — remove them before building. The painted lines on the ground are your reference.

  • After grading: Remove all strings. Paint lines may get covered — remark them if needed.
  • Keep corner stakes: Leave A, B, C, D in place until frame is assembled. They help you eyeball square.
  • Remove before decking: All stakes come out once frame is up and level. Don't build over them.
