Pool Deck Stake & String Layout

Site Prep Guide — v8.1 LOCKED · May 14, 2026
🎯 Goal: Mark the exact deck footprint on the ground before any digging or grading. Uses only stakes, string, a tape measure, and a helper. No fancy tools needed.

2Find Your Reference Point

Every measurement starts from one fixed point. For this deck, the best reference is the pool coping (the rim). It's already level, round, and won't move.

💡 Mark the center of the pool first · Measure across the coping in 2 directions (like a + sign) · Where they cross = center · Drive a temporary stake · All radial measurements start here

4Step-by-Step Layout

Step 1: Mark the Back Edge (Stake A → Stake B)

1
Decide where the back of the deck goes. This is where the step will be. Usually ~15' from the pool coping.
2
Drive Stake A at left corner of back edge. Use mallet until firm.
3
Measure 13' exactly to the right. Drive Stake B. Tie string A→B tight at ground level.
4
Verify: String should be straight, taut, touching ground. Spray paint along string for visible line.

Step 2: Mark the Left & Right Sides (Perpendicular from A & B)

5
At Stake A, use the 3-4-5 triangle method to find a perpendicular line:
  • Measure 3' along string A→B, mark with tape or thumb
  • From that mark, measure 4' out into yard (away from pool)
  • Adjust until the diagonal = exactly 5'
  • That's your 90° angle — direction of left side
6
Drive Stake D at ~11.5' from A along that perpendicular. Tie string A→D.
7
Repeat at Stake B — 3-4-5 triangle right side. Drive Stake C at ~11.5'. Tie string B→C.
💡 Pro tip: Instead of 3-4-5, you can also use a framing square or measure diagonals: A→C and B→D should be exactly equal (both ~17' 3"). If they're not, your rectangle isn't square.

Step 3: Mark the Transition Line (Ledger Location)

8
Measure 11.5' from string A→B (back edge) toward the pool. Mark with spray paint across the full 13' width.
9
This is your 2×4 ledger line — the transition between main deck (behind) and curved section (ahead).
10
Drive two temporary stakes along this line (left and right) and tie a string. This string will guide curved joist placement later.

Step 4: Mark the Curved Front Edge

11
From your pool center stake, tie a string that's 12'6" long (12' pool radius + ~6" for overhang/offset).
12
Swing this string in an arc across the front of the deck area. Mark the ground with spray paint every 6–12". This is your curved deck edge.
13
The curve should intersect the left/right side strings (A→D and B→C) at points about 3.5' from the transition line. This is your check — if it's way off, re-verify pool center.
⚠️ Double-check: The curved edge should be roughly parallel to the pool coping, about 1" outside it. Step back and eyeball it — if it looks wrong, it probably is.

5Pedestal Layout (Where the Feet Go)

6Grading & Site Prep

What to Remove

  • Grass and topsoil (first 2–3 inches)
  • Roots, rocks, stumps inside the footprint
  • Anything organic that will decompose and settle

What to Add

  • Crushed stone: 2–3 tons of ¾" stone, spread and tamp with hand tamper or plate compactor
  • Flat stone pavers: One under each pedestal location (more stable than gravel alone)
  • Combo: Flat stone at corners + high-traffic spots, gravel in between

Level Check

1
Lay a straight 2×6 across the footprint in multiple directions.
2
Check with a level. Goal: ±1" across entire 13' × ~15' area. Slight slope away from pool for drainage is OK.
3
If low spots: add stone and tamp. If high spots: dig out or scrape down.
4
Tamp everything firm. Walk on it — should feel solid, not spongy.
⚠️ Critical: The stone base is what carries the load. Pedestals on soft ground = deck sinks. Take your time here.

7Removing Strings & Stakes

Strings are temporary — remove them before building. The painted lines on the ground are your reference.

  • After grading: Remove all strings. Paint lines may get covered — remark them if needed.
  • Keep corner stakes: Leave A, B, C, D in place until frame is assembled. They help you eyeball square.
  • Remove before decking: All stakes come out once frame is up and level. Don't build over them.
