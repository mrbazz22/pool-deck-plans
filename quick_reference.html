2Structure

Support

  • NO posts in ground · Freestanding on pedestals
  • 38× Eurotec ECO M + L (various heights)
  • Base: flat stone pavers, compacted gravel, or combo
  • 1–2 precast concrete bases at corners (optional)

Framing

  • NO doubled beam · Joists sit directly on pedestals/stone
  • Doubled 2×6 rims all around each section (two boards face-screwed)
  • Field joists: 2×6, 16" O.C., 11.5' full length, front-to-back · ~8 per section
  • Border blocking: 2×6 pieces every 16" O.C. under all double-row borders · NEW for v11.1
  • Mid-span blocking: 2×6 at ~5.75' from back (one row per section)
  • Center seam: 6" gap between sections · Doubled rims on inner edges
  • Diagonal bracing: 2×4 cross-bracing between field joists at corners/mid-span · Prevents racking

Decking Direction

  • Inner field: Runs left→right (parallel to back edge) · 9' 2" span per section
  • Double borders: Run perpendicular (front-to-back on sides, left→right on front/back)
  • Center seam: 6" wide strip runs front-to-back (perpendicular to field)
  • Border blocking supports perpendicular border boards under border zones

Expansion Gaps (Georgia Climate)

  • 5/4×6 main deck: 1/8"–3/16" between boards
  • 2×6 curved section: 3/16"–¼" between boards
  • End gaps at rim: ½" (allows board movement)
  • Dark stain absorbs heat → boards expand more → err slightly wider

Inspector Notes

  • Freestanding = no ledger hold-downs required
  • Georgia inspectors may require hurricane ties or strap ties at outer rim · Check before build
  • 36" railing height is residential standard

4Stain Checklist

💡 All stain supplies on hand: 5 gal BEHR Stonehedge, 9" roller + covers, 3" brush, 2" edge brush, painter's tape, drop cloths
1
Pressure wash all boards · Let dry 48+ hrs
2
Label every piece with position (e.g., "DECK-ROW-3") · Painter's tape + marker
3
End grain first: Heavy coat on all cut ends · Double coat · Let dry 30 min
4
Roll faces: 9" roller, ¼" nap · Work in sections · Maintain wet edge
5
Back-brush: 3" quality brush · Work stain into cracks · Hand-finished look
6
Flip & repeat: Dry 4–6 hrs, flip, stain back faces and edges
7
Second coat (optional): After 24 hrs · Two thin > one heavy
💡 Morning shade = friend · Hot sun dries stain too fast · Work early or in shade

6Build Order

1
Site Prep: Clear · Grade ±1" · Stone/gravel base · Layout pedestals for both sections
2
Stain: All 6 faces before assembly · 2–3 days · Dry weather
3
Frame Section A: Doubled 2×6 rims · ~8 field joists 16" O.C. (11.5' length) · On pedestals · Level to ~7"
4
Frame Section B: Same as A · Leave 6" gap between sections · Doubled inner rims on both sides
5
Blocking: Mid-span blocking at ~5.75' · Border blocking every 16" O.C. under all double-row borders (all sides of both sections) · Center seam blocking
6
Joist Tape: Apply to all joist tops, rim tops, and blocking tops before decking
7
Inner Field Decking: 5/4×6 left→right · 1/8"–3/16" gaps · 2.5" screws · Two per joist · Cut from 12' stock = ~9'6" use + ~2'6" offcut
8
Double Borders: Row 1: perpendicular pieces along all sides · Row 2: second row completing 11" depth · 3/16" gaps · Use offcuts from field boards
9
Center Seam: 6" wide perpendicular strip between sections · Cut from remaining offcuts
10
Railings + Step: 6×6 posts · 2×6 rails · 36" height · Box step on back edge · 7" rise
