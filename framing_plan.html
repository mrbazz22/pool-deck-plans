Blocking Plan

Blocking runs perpendicular to joists (leftвҶ’right), at mid-span (~5.75' from back edge). This prevents the 11.5' joists from twisting and adds stiffness.

  • One row of blocking at ~5.75' from back edge
  • 16 pieces total (one per bay between joists)
  • 15 pieces: ~14.5" long (16" bay minus 1.5" joist width)
  • 1 piece: ~10.5" (edge bay) long (12" edge bay minus 1.5" joist width)
  • Cut from the 1' back rim offcut + any 14' offcuts you have
  • Face-screw to joists with 2.5" exterior screws
вҡ пёҸ Ledger Support
The 2Г—4 ledger (22') needs solid backing where the 2Г—4 curved joists attach. Screw the ledger to the ends of the 2Г—6 field joists AND add short 2Г—6 blocking pieces between joist ends where the ledger spans. Use 3" screws, 2 per connection.

05Summary: Build Day Cut Order

Stain everything first, then cut. Here's the order:

  1. Back rim: Splice two 14' 2Г—6s to 22' with 6" overlap, or use 11.5' + 11.5' with blocking. Offcuts вҶ’ blocking.
  2. Side rims: Use two ~11.5' 2Г—6s at full length. NO cuts needed!
  3. Field joists: Use nine ~11.5' 2Г—6s at full length. NO cuts needed!
  4. Blocking: From offcut pile, cut 10 blocking pieces: nine at ~14.5", one at ~10.5".
  5. Ledger: Splice two 14' 2Г—4s to 22' with 6" overlap and screws, or buy 24' stock.
  6. Decking: Set aside 24 full 12' boards for main field. Cut 3 boards into 24Г— ~1' pieces for landing strip.
  7. Curved joists: Cut 2Г—4s to fan lengths: 1Г— ~6.5', ~6Г— 2'вҖ“3', 2Г— ~2'.
вҡ пёҸ Don't Forget
All cuts get stain on the fresh end grain BEFORE assembly. End grain drinks stain вҖ” hit it twice. The offcuts for blocking also need their ends sealed if they'll be exposed.
