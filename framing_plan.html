012×6 Framing Plan

Each section is framed independently as an 11' × 11.5' rectangle. Joists run front-to-back at full 11.5' length — no cuts. Doubled 2×6 rims on all four sides. The key win: zero joist cuts and identical sections.

💡 Doubled Rims for Strength
All four rim joists are doubled 2×6 (two boards face-screwed together). This makes the frame beefier, gives the border blocking something solid to anchor to, and matches the substantial look of the railings.

💡 The Big Win: Zero Joist Cuts
All field joists and side rims use full stock length. Only the back/front rims need cutting (11' from 14' stock). The 3' offcuts become perfect blocking stock.
💡 Joist Spacing Math
11' = 132". At 16" O.C. with rim on both sides: joists at 0, 16, 32, 48, 64, 80, 96, 112, 128, and rim at 132". That's ~8–9 joists per section (6–7 field + 2 rim). Clean, simple numbers.

Blocking Plan

Mid-Span Blocking

One row of 2×6 blocking at ~5.75' from back edge of each section. Prevents the 11.5' joists from twisting and adds stiffness.

  • ~8 pieces per section (one per bay between field joists)
  • ~14–18" long (bay width minus 1.5" joist width)
  • Cut from 3' rim offcuts or scrap 2×6
  • Face-screw to joists with 2.5" exterior screws

Border Blocking (Picture-Frame Support)

Critical support for perpendicular border boards. Installed every 16" O.C. under all border zones.

  • Back edge: 2×6 pieces running left→right, every 16" O.C. · ~8 pieces per section · Screwed to back rim and field joist ends
  • Front edge: 2×6 pieces running left→right, every 16" O.C. · ~8 pieces per section
  • Left/right sides: 2×6 pieces running front-to-back, every 16" O.C. · ~6 pieces per side per section · ~12 per section total
  • Total per section: ~28–30 border blocking pieces · ~60 total
  • Length: ~14–18" each · Cut from 2×6 scrap or rim offcuts

Center Seam Blocking

Supports the short perpendicular boards spanning the 6" gap between sections.

  • ~5 pieces per side of seam = ~10 total (spaced every 16" O.C. along 11.5' depth)
  • Each piece ~6" long · Cut from 2×6 scrap
  • Screwed to inner rim joists of both sections

💡 Decking Row Count Math
Inner field is 9' 6" deep = 114". Each 5/4×6 board is ~5.5" wide + ¼" gap = 5.75" effective. 114" ÷ 5.75" = ~19.8 rows. Adjust gap slightly (⅛"–¼") to land clean without ripping the last board. Border rows are 11" deep = 2 rows of 5.5" boards.
💡 Border Piece Yield from Offcuts
Each 12' field board yields one ~9' 6" field piece + one ~2' 6" offcut. Each ~2' 6" offcut yields ~2 border pieces (~11" each). ⚠️ Do Not Skip Border Blocking
Without blocking, each ~11" border piece is only supported at one end (the rim). The free end will flex under foot traffic, eventually crack, and always squeak. The 2×6 blocking takes 10 minutes per section and makes the border feel as solid as the field.

05Summary: Build Day Cut Order

Stain everything first, then cut. Here's the order:

  1. Back/front rims: Cut four 14' 2×6s to 11' each (2 back + 2 front per section). Offcuts (~3' each = 8 pieces) → blocking stock.
  2. Side rims: Use four ~11.5' 2×6s at full length (2 per section). NO cuts needed!
  3. Field joists: Use ~16 ~11.5' 2×6s at full length (~8 per section). NO cuts needed!
  4. Mid-span blocking: From offcut pile, cut ~16 pieces: ~14–18" each.
  5. Border blocking: From offcuts/scrap, cut ~56 pieces: ~14–18" each.
  6. Center seam blocking: From scrap, cut ~10 pieces: ~6" each.
  7. Field decking: Set aside ~32 full 12' boards. Cut each to ~9' 6". Save ~2' 6" offcuts for border stock.
  8. Border decking: Cut offcuts and ~8–10 extra boards into ~11" border pieces. Need ~188 per section × 2 = ~376 total? Recheck: ~14 extra boards × ~13 pieces each = ~182. Plus ~64 from offcuts = ~246. Need ~376. Hmm, adjust: use ~20 extra boards. Or accept that some border pieces come from boards dedicated to border stock. Recalculate with actual math at build time.
⚠️ Don't Forget
All cuts get stain on the fresh end grain BEFORE assembly. End grain drinks stain — hit it twice. The offcuts for blocking also need their ends sealed if they'll be exposed.
